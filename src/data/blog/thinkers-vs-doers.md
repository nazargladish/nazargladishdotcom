There are two types of people: thinkers and doers.

When there is an idea that can be turned into action, people's first reaction is either:

* Contemplate the idea
* Implement the idea

Contemplators often find themselves paralyzed by the thought, or they just enjoy the process, procrastinating on action (fear of failure), or it's how their mind is wired based on their previous reactions. Thinking is good - as long as it's not excessive. Action should prevail and become a habit, an internalised reaction, and then your default response. "I am the one who manifests an idea" - that should be your core identity.

The doers will not think about ideas much, they won't overthink or try to plan it thoroughly - usually those plans run into harsh, indifferent reality anyway and need to be reconsidered.

Reality is your teacher, not your mind or imagination. It's fascinating, how far you can go if you just start doing things, every time. Once you have internalized the "doers" mindset, you will become quick to act.

When you find a good idea, it's imperative not to hesitate and act on it. For thinkers, it's harder to go and just build an imperfect, incomplete, or under-planned product. In their mind, the initiative looks **"too big"** to be done quickly, because that's how their brain was trained.

Therefore, it arguably always leads to a situation where doers test more ideas, and outpace thinkers. Time is important - so many opportunities are lost because of an endless search for a better option. Just take what you have and go!

Because doers test more hypotheses, by sheer probability, they have higher probability of success.

Being able to quickly build something nice and put it in front of people is a much greater feeling than having it just in your brain.

Set yourself free and just do it.
